Useless Facts about Presidents

  1. Only President to remain a bachelor: James Buchanon
  2. Only first lady to carry a loaded revolver: Eleanor Roosevelt
  3. Only President to win a Pulitzer: John F. Kennedy for Profiles in Courage
  4. Only President awarded a patent: Abe Lincoln, for a system of buoying vessels over shoals
  5. Thomas Jefferson, John Adams, and James Monroe all died on July 4th. Jefferson and Adams died at practically the same minute of the same day.
  6. Franklin Pierce is the only President to have said "I promise" instead of "I swear at his Inauguration. He did it for religious reasons
  7. At Andrew Jackson's funeral in 1845, his pet parrot had to be removed because it was swearing
  8. Andrew Jackson is the only President to have also been held as a prisoner of war. This was during the Revolutionary War
  9. When George Washington was elected President, there was a king in France, a czarina in Russia, an emperor in China, and a shogun in Japan. Only the office of President remains
  10. Andrew Johnson, the 17th President, was the only President to make his own clothes as well as his cabinet's
  11. There were two Presidents whose names contained double letters in both their first and last names: William Harrison and Millard Fillmore
  12. Harrison was the only President born in the same county as his vice president
  13. When the Hoovers did not want to be overheard by White House guests, they spoke to each other in Chinese
  14. Lyndon Johnson loved the soda Fresca so much he had a fountain installed in the Oval Office that dispensed the beverage, which the president could operate by pushing a button on his desk chair
  15. All U.S. Presidents have worn glasses, some of them just didn't like to be seen with them in public
  16. Gerald Ford was once a male model
  17. President James Garfield could write Latin with one hand and Greek with the other
  18. Four of the first six U.S. Presidents were age 57 when they were inaugurated. Washington, Jefferson, Madison and Adams. They were also the last U.S. Presidents to be inaugurated at that age
  19. Each American President has had a personal piano, with the exception of Gerald Ford and George Bush
  20. Only 4 Presidents served their entire terms without having a Vice President (before the 25th Amerndment which provided for filling vacancies in the Vice Presidency): Tyler, Fillmore, Andrew Johnson, and Arthur
  21. No president has ever died in the month of May
  22. Washington carried a portable sundial
  23. Washington once issued an order that forbade swearing throughout the U.S. Army
  24. James Polk survived a gallstone operation at age 17 without anethesia or antiseptics
